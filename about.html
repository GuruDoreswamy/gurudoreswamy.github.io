About Me

Engineering leadership, digital transformation, and AI-driven systems

Senior automotive engineering professional specialising in product and program management, digital transformation, and leading AI-driven transformation across engineering systems. With two decades of experience, I work on vehicle body structure concept development, analytical modeling, and technical validation, including finite element modeling for NVH, crash, and safety attributes, and shape morphing during early phase of vehicle architecture.I lead complex product programs from early concept phase through validation combining deep technical expertise with systems-level leadership.

As my career evolved, I transitioned from delivering individual engineering solutions to shaping and governing the systems, processes, and platforms through which engineering decisions are made. Along with product development projects, I now lead automation, digital toolchain modernization, and AI-driven initiatives that transform expert-driven workflows into structured, scalable, and data-informed decision systems across the organization.

I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering and an MBA in Business Analytics (Work-Integrated Learning Program). I am certified in Product Management (ISB, Hyderabad) and hold professional credentials as a Certified Scrum Product Owner (CSPO), ISO/TS 16949 Internal Auditor, and Six Sigma Green Belt (Ford).

I am known for Service delivery excellence, leading large cross-functional teams, and building strong, trust-based partnerships with customers and suppliers. My focus is consistently on delivering measurable business value—whether through process automation, cost reduction, digital toolchain modernization, or AI-enabled initiatives implemented at enterprise scale.

On a day-to-day basis, I lead a team of 25 engineers responsible for developing automotive body structure concepts for future vehicle programs. Working at the concept phase, we translate early design intent into robust, simulation-backed solutions for next-generation products—ensuring the work remains technically challenging, forward-looking, and deeply engaging.
